page one — the messy bit

I failed 10th. Then I failed Inter.

Both times the real reason was money. Final year exams — we hadn't paid the fees. We went and asked, explained the situation, somehow got a hall ticket. I sat the exam. Passed. The certificate is still sitting in that college office. We just never had the ₹3,000 to go collect it. So technically, no degree. Not because I didn't try — just that even getting proof you passed, costs money we didn't have.

Bad quality. Zero views. Still posted it. That's literally the whole lesson.

So I just kept going. Uploaded whatever made sense that day. 0 views. 3 views. 7 views on a good week. Paid one guy to coach me — he just said things I already knew but louder. Tried every "grow fast" shortcut I could find on YouTube. Bought fake subscribers once. Watched a number go up that meant nothing.

Four years. Not one rupee came in.

What finally changed — it wasn't posting more, or working harder, I was already doing both. It was just one thing: actually understanding why someone watches a video till the end. That's it. ₹17 lakhs in the 9 months after I figured that out.

★ the unhinged one

I also just like testing my own limits.

GOGGINS RECORD: 4,030
MY ATTEMPT: 2,800

David Goggins holds the world record for most pull-ups in 17 hours — 4,030. I decided to try it live on YouTube. Under 1,000 people watched. That was fine. I wasn't doing it for the views.

I got to 2,800 before my bicep started tearing. Stopped. Not the record. Still the most pull-ups I've ever seen anyone in my city attempt in one sitting. I don't regret it.
