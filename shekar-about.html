page one — the messy bit

I'm not going to dress this up. I failed my Intermediate (12th). Badly enough that we couldn't pay the final college fees — my marksheet and transfer certificate are still sitting with that college, unclaimed. No money for a degree, and my family didn't have it either. So I just... stopped. I wasn't good at studying anyway. What was the point?

I was 17, broke, with one phone — an Oppo A37 — and a lot of free time I didn't know what to do with. My brother was the one who said it: "just try YouTube, what's the worst that happens." So we go there.

That first video is genuinely bad. No plan, no idea what a niche even was. Four years of posting and I made not even one rupee. I tried every "content coach," every monetization hack channel, even tried fake subscribers once out of pure desperation — nothing worked. Looking back, none of it was ever going to.

It was only after I slowed down on @thinknwear_ that things actually clicked — really understanding content instead of chasing hacks. In the last 9 months alone, that understanding turned into ₹17L+. The four years before that taught me everything the 9 months needed.

I also just like testing my own limits.

David Goggins holds the world record for most pull-ups in 17 hours — 4,030. I went live on YouTube and tried to attempt it myself. No real expectation anyone in India would care about a pull-up world record attempt, and honestly, barely anyone did — under 1,000 views. That was fine. I wasn't doing it for the views.

I chased it until my body said stop — a minor bicep tear ended it at 2,800 pull-ups. Not the record. Still the most pull-ups I've ever seen anyone around me even attempt.
